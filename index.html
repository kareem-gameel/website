About

Building physical understanding into predictive models.

I develop first-principles quantum chemistry and scientific machine-learning methods for electronic response, nanoscopic interfaces, and materials discovery.

My goal is to bridge theory and experiment by identifying the physics that governs nanoscopic phenomena and translating that understanding into predictive computational models that accelerate discovery.

Research program

Two connected questions

How does electronic structure govern measurable response at an interface, and how should a learning problem be designed so that its predictions transfer across chemical space?

01

Electronic Response at Nanoscopic Interfaces

Interfaces determine how charge and fields move through batteries, supercapacitors, catalysts, sensors, and semiconductor devices. I develop first-principles descriptions of field-induced charge redistribution, quantum capacitance, and electrostatic screening that connect atomic structure to measurable device response.

02

Generalizable ML for Molecular and Materials Discovery

Generalization depends on learning mappings that remain smooth across chemical space. I study how physical representations, model expressivity, low-cost baselines, and target transformations can align a learning problem with its underlying physics and improve transfer beyond the training distribution.

Research Projects

A research program connecting first-principles response theory, physical target design, and electronic-structure-informed machine learning.

01 · First-principles interfaces

Interfacial Capacitance at the Nanoscale

Extending the Quantum Interfacial Capacitance framework across substrate-2D interfaces and confined electrode geometries to resolve how screening, electronic structure, and ion confinement shape measurable capacitance.

Interface paper ↗ Confinement paper ↗

02 · Target design

Delta Learning for Transferable Molecular Models

Treating delta learning as target design: selecting physical baselines that remove difficult target components and leave smoother residuals aligned with a model's inductive bias.

Manuscript in progress

03 · Electronic representation

Hamiltonian-Informed Electronic Structure Learning

Combining Hamiltonian-derived descriptors with atomistic geometry to learn bandgaps and spectral quantities governed by nonlocal electronic coupling.
